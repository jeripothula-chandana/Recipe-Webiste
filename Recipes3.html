Preparation Time

  • Total: 2.5 hours
  • Preparation: 1 hour
  • Cooking: 1.5 hours

Ingredients

  • Mutton - 500 g
  • Basmati rice - 2 cups
  • Yogurt - 1 cup
  • Onions - 3 large, sliced
  • Tomatoes - 2, chopped
  • Green chilies - 4
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 2 tbsp
  • Spices: bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom
  • Biryani masala - 2 tbsp
  • Saffron - a pinch (soaked in milk)
  • Ghee (clarified butter) - 3 tbsp
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves - 1/2 cup each
  • Salt - to taste

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak the rice for 30 minutes. Cook until 70% done and set aside.
  2. Marinate the mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, and salt. Let it rest for 1 hour.
  3. Heat ghee in a large pot. Fry sliced onions until golden brown. Set aside some for garnishing.
  4. Add whole spices (bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom) to the ghee, followed by marinated mutton. Cook until the mutton is tender.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, and fresh herbs (mint and coriander). Cook until the oil separates.
  6. In a large pot, layer the mutton gravy and half-cooked rice. Drizzle saffron milk, fried onions, and ghee on top.
  7. Cover tightly and cook on low flame (dum) for 30-40 minutes.
  8. Serve hot with raita and garnish with fresh herbs.
